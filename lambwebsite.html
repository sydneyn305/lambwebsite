About lambs

Lamb is the term used to refer to the newborn offspring of domesticated sheep. On average, only one or two lambs are born to a ewe each year. Sheep are a medium-sized, hoofed mammal that were domesticated some 10,000 ago by humans for their wool, skin, milk and meat. They belong to a group of animals called ruminants, which means that their stomach have chambers that contain microbes that helps them digest their forages of the day.
